Nutrition

Background/Objectives

Malnutrition is a prevalent problem in patients with cancer and is associated with poor prognosis. The European Society for Clinical Nutrition and Metabolism recommends the provision of nutritional support and evaluation. This study aims to describe the nutritional management of patients with terminal cancer from the perspective of both patients and healthcare professionals.

Subjects/Methods

A cross-sectional descriptive observational study was carried out through two surveys addressed to patients and HCPs.


Conclusions

A study has shown that in Spain the nutritional approach is suboptimal in terms of screening/assessment of malnutrition and information provided to patients.

Results

More than half (55.0%) of cancer patients experience eating problems during treatment and 64.0% reported sequelae affecting nourishment. 60.1% of patients and 42.6% of HCPs indicated that nutritional status is not generally assessed. General discomfort or gastrointestinal problems were the main issues hampering correct adherence.
